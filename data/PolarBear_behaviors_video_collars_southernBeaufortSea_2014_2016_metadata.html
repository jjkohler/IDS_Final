Description:
Abstract:
This dataset contains time-linked behavior data derived from video camera collars on southern Beaufort Sea polar bears. Nine solitary female polar bears were equipped with video-camera collars (Exeye, LLC., Bristow, VA) for 8 - 11 days in April, 2014-2016.
Purpose:
These data were collected to gain insights into the spring activity and foraging success rates of female polar bears without dependent young in the southern Beaufort Sea.

Methodology:
Methodology_Type: Field
Methodology_Description:
In 2014, 4 video cameras recorded for 1 min every 2 min between 4 pm and 9 pm (UTC) and for 30 sec every 2 min between 9 pm and 5 am (UTC). In 2015, 1 video camera recorded for 1 min every 2 min between 4 pm and 9 pm (UTC) and for 30 sec every 2 min between 9 pm and 6 am (UTC) and 2 video cameras recorded for 1 min every 2 min between 4 pm and 12 am (UTC) and for 30 sec every 2 min between 12 am and 6 am (UTC). In 2016, 2 video cameras recorded for 1 min every 2 min between 4 pm and 6 am (UTC). All 9 video cameras were programmed to turn off if the temperature of the collar was ≤ -17 °C to protect video equipment.

Process_Step:
Process_Description:
We collected video data from 9 female polar bears of the southern Beaufort Sea in April 2014-2016 over 8-11 days. Video collars recorded a total per bear of 14 - 58 hours of video while on bears.
Process_Date: Unknown
Process_Step:
Process_Description:
Behaviors and the times in which they began and ended were annotated based on time-stamped videos recorded via the video camera collar. Behaviors in 2014 were discriminated at 30 second/1 minute resolutions. Behaviors in 2015 and 2016 were dicriminated at 1 second resolutions.
